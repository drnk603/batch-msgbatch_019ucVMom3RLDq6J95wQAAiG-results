Cookie Policy

Last Updated:

This Cookie Policy explains how domain ("we", "us", or "our"), operating at domain.com, uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website. By continuing to use our site, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy. This policy is compliant with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the ePrivacy Directive.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to website owners.

Cookies allow a website to recognize your device, remember your preferences, and improve your overall browsing experience. Some cookies are deleted when you close your browser (session cookies), while others remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them (persistent cookies).

We may also use similar technologies such as web beacons, pixel tags, and local storage that function similarly to cookies. Throughout this policy, we refer to all of these technologies collectively as "cookies."

5. Cookie Retention Periods

The duration for which cookies remain on your device varies depending on the type of cookie:

  • Session Cookies: These are temporary cookies that are deleted automatically when you close your browser. They are typically used for authentication and form data.
  • Persistent Cookies (Short-Term): These cookies remain on your device for up to 30 days. They are typically used for remembering preferences across short intervals.
  • Persistent Cookies (Medium-Term): These cookies remain on your device for 3 to 6 months. They are often used by analytics and advertising tools.
  • Persistent Cookies (Long-Term): These cookies remain on your device for up to 2 years. Google Analytics uses long-term cookies to track unique users over time.
  • Consent Cookies: Our cookie consent record is stored for 12 months before we ask for your preferences again.

You can delete cookies at any time using your browser settings. Deleting cookies does not prevent new cookies from being placed when you next visit our website.

6. How to Manage Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing and controlling cookies on your device. Please note that restricting certain cookies may impact the functionality of our website.

6.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can configure your browser to block all cookies, accept only certain cookies, or notify you when a cookie is set. Refer to your browser's help documentation for instructions:

  • Google Chrome — Cookie Settings
  • Mozilla Firefox — Cookie Settings
  • Apple Safari — Cookie Settings
  • Microsoft Edge — Cookie Settings

6.2 Opt-Out Tools

You can also opt out of specific third-party cookies using the following tools:

  • Google Analytics Opt-Out: Install the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-On
  • Meta (Facebook) Opt-Out: Manage your Facebook Ad Preferences
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA): optout.aboutads.info
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI): optout.networkadvertising.org

6.3 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You may update your preferences at any time by clearing your browser's cookies, which will cause the consent banner to reappear on your next visit.

7. Your Consent

In accordance with the GDPR and the ePrivacy Directive (Cookie Law), we obtain your consent before placing non-essential cookies on your device. Specifically:

  • Necessary cookies are placed without consent as they are strictly required for the website to function.
  • Analytical, marketing, and functional cookies are only placed after you have given your explicit, informed consent via our cookie consent banner.

You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of cookie use prior to withdrawal. To withdraw consent, you can:

  • Clear all cookies in your browser settings and decline when the consent banner reappears.
  • Use your browser's private/incognito mode for sessions without persistent cookies.
  • Contact us directly at sales@domain.com to request cookie records deletion.

We record your consent decision along with a timestamp and the version of this Cookie Policy that was in effect at the time of consent. This record is retained for compliance purposes.

8. GDPR & ePrivacy Compliance

Our cookie practices are designed to comply with the following regulations:

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — EU 2016/679: We process personal data collected through cookies on the legal bases of consent (Article 6(1)(a)) and legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) where applicable.
  • ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC as amended): We obtain prior informed consent before storing or accessing information on your terminal equipment, except where strictly necessary.
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA): California residents have the right to opt out of the sale of personal information. We do not sell personal data. You may still limit data sharing for targeted advertising purposes.

As a data controller, domain is responsible for the cookies we set directly. For cookies set by third parties, those providers act as independent data controllers under their own policies.
